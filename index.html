What Is shadeDB?

Developers often ask — what the hell’s shadeDB and why would anyone choose it over PostgreSQL?

shadeDB — not the released PyPI beta — began as a personal solution for data control during the development of shadecreed. I realized JSON was too limited: it couldn’t handle duplicate keys. So, I wrote a small storage engine that could store key–value pairs, even duplicates, and retrieve them efficiently.

It took nearly five weeks to finalize the first version. It worked, but performance wasn’t impressive — compiling 36MB of disk took 51 seconds, and queries averaged 2 seconds each.

That inefficiency sparked a new mission — to build a faster, lighter, memory-conscious system that could compete with and outperform traditional databases.

Why Choose shadeDB?

Here are key reasons developers are already keeping an eye on shadeDB:

  • 1. Lower Latency — shadeDB achieves an average latency of 0.3ms while consuming less than 1KB of memory per query.
  • 2. Cross-Platform Availability — Seamless integration into IoT, robotics, web apps, ML models, and machine systems with 98% uptime.
  • 3. Data Ownership — Emphasizing user-driven control, shadeDB will reward early contributors to the network.
  • 4. Distributed Computational Power — Temporary encrypted data storage on virtual node servers ensures uptime even when offline.
  • 5. Vertical Store Receipt System — Full transparency and rollback tracking through isolated disk partitions.
  • 6. Easy Setup — Fully autonomous configuration and optimization — ready in minutes.
